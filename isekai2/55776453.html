The guards standing at both sides of the southern gate leading into Ringstad were busy passing their shift kicking a stone back and forth between them. The south gate saw the least traffic of the three gates into the city, being closest to the coast and furthest from the main street, so usually only the old or the slow were sent to stand guard there.

One of the guards noticed a person approaching out of the corner of his eye, and looked up from the game he had entertained himself with. He flinched, rubbing his eyes before doing a double take. Coming up the seaside road was a man the size of an oxen, draped in a leather cloak covering his body from head to feet. His large beard was obscuring most of face, but clear, strong eyes were gazing sternly out from underneath his hood. “Halt! Who goes there?” the guardsman called out, instinctively moving his hand to the hilt of his sword. The large man did not break his stride, but instead raised a hand from the inside of his cloak. His bare, muscled arm was the size of a cart axle, and covered in scars.
